How does different country celebrate Thanksgiving?

Different countries have their own way of celebrating Thanksgiving. Countries like Germany celebrate Thanksgiving on a different date rather than in November. There are countries who does not celebrate Thanksgiving like Brazil (even though they share American's Thanksgiving Holiday).

Germany

Japan

In Japan, they celebrate Thanksgiving Labor Day for Thanksgiving. It also means that they don't feast like other countries do. The festival is celebrated every 23rd of November since 1868. The festival is celebrated by organizations leading events where citizens celebrate workers of their hard work and as a community. Children during the festival make a thank-you cards for workers who work in services who are municipal workers.

Grenada

Grenada celebrates Thanksgiving every October 25th since 1983. The tradition happened because they want to celebrate the American and Caribbean internvention in their nation to overthrow their military dictator. To show their gratitude, they invited soldiers to feast with on Thanksgiving.

Ghana

Ghana celebrates Thanksgiving through Homowo festival. Homowo festival is paying tribute to the Ga tribe's ancestors during a famine centuries ago. During the time Ga tribe arrived on Ghana's coast, the soil was not fertile and there was no rain for a long period of time. When rain happened, the Ga people had a loud celebration for the huge blessing of food. In translation, Homowo means hooting at hunger which represent the Ga people during the famine. In addition to their tradition, Ghana's capitical city of Accra enforces a noise ban for a month to reflect on the Ga people who died in the famine time including fishing in the lagoons to represent the lack of food. After a month, the noise ban is lifted following with church service honoring the deceased and servicing special dishes which includes a cornmeal called kpokpoi.
