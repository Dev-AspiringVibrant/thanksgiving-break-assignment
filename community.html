Wellness during Thanksgiving

A Time to be Thankful

During Thanksgiving, you can do many activities to boost your mental health. Here are some activities you can
do to keep your mental health strong.

What are you thankful for?

First, grab a journal. Before you write anything, you must first think about what made you happy. Then, write
what comes to mind. Could it be family, helping others, doing something you like? Next, reflect on that memory.
Why did that moment make you happy? What made that moment important to you? List any significance about
your life or anything related to that memory.

Spending Time with Friends or Family

It is the perfect time to invite friends or family. You can prepare food and dine with your loved ones. You can also
hang out and play with close ones. The activities you can do with them can be small and big. It can be from playing
games at home or participating in a community event.

Going Out

Grab a Seasonal Item

Some places have special offer for a limited amount of time. For instance, a flavor of a coffee drink at a cafe can
be taken off the menu and you would have to wait for a year to come by around.

Going to an Event

You can go participate to a local community event and socialize with other people. By going to a community event,
you can connect with others.There are activities can play or do such as growing a pumpkin and competing in having
the largest possible pumpkin amongst your competitor and earning a medal for winning, doing food drive for those
who need food or to support the cause of food bank, or watch or participate in a Thanksgiving show.

Last but not least...

You can do all of these activities at once to get the most out of it! First, you gather your friends and family to get
a time-limited Thanksgiving item. Then, you can go to a fun local community event and celebrate
together along with other people who attended the event. After the event is over, write down in your
journal what made this day special.
